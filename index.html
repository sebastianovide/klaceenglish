The Klace Difference

Our program is for High school students from age 12 onwards. Our two-week program, based in London, and begins each summer from July, immerses students in practical, English-speaking environments and offers experiential-based learning, including drama, debating, storytelling, public speaking and cultural immersion

Our program is based on a simple idea - learning by doing. And our results are proven. Unlike most typical English development courses, Klace allows students to take their classroom teachings to the next level. Students from diverse cultures are placed into exciting environments full of new experiences. Our two-week program provides total cultural immersion including exciting field excursions and hosting by English speaking families.

To further suport our teaching methodologies, each classroom is equipped with the latest technological equipment, including a SMART Board. This innovative tool has been likened to an interactive whiteboard. Audio can easily be embedded into SMART Notebook lessons reinforces important or difficult vocabulary and expressions in an interactive manner. The SMART Response system gauges student comprehension of topics covered.

Market

Kingston’s historic Market Place has been in use since around 1170 when Henry II was on the throne. Over the past 800 years Market Place has been used for much more than just selling produce. It has been the focal point for outdoor entertainment and activities in Kingston today and, just like in ancient times.

River Thames

Kingston is set in beautiful surroundings and the picturesque River Thames, the second longest river in the UK, is right on the doorstep. The river cuts a charming route through the borough for almost three miles, connecting Kingston to Hampton Court, Richmond and central London. Many of Kingston’s annual events are focused on the river including the annual Kingston Regatta and River Festival.

Hampton Court Palace

Hampton Court Palace is a royal palace in the London Borough of Richmond upon Thames, Greater London, in the historic countyof Middlesex, and within the postal town East Molesey, Surrey; it has not been inhabited by the British Royal Family since the 18th century. The palace is 11.7 miles (18.8 kilometres) south west of Charing Cross and upstream of central London on the River Thames.

Curriculum

Klace's curriculum includes a well-balanced program of experienced-based learning activities, off-site excursions, and classroom studies. Experiential content includes drama exercises, storytelling, public speaking and debating. Exciting field excursions include visits to local parks, cafes and entertainment venues. These activities provide total cultural immersion, taking what the students learn from our interactive activities and the classroom and put their skills to practical use in real-world situations. And students find our classrooms spacious and bright with class size not exceeding 16 students.

As the main difference of Klace's Curriculum, our experiential course work allows students to do a deep dive into using English in ways normal classroom environments simply can't provide.

Weekly activities*

Theatre

Theatrical Icebreaking with a mini script development and group performances develop voice and English comprehension skills. Through this activity, students practice fluency and further enhance their comprehension skills

Storytelling

Expression of thought by reading, listening, and practicing English language sounds in a fun way. Storytelling helps students learn to take their thoughts and use English to express ideas and increase their conversational skills.

Public Speaking

Turning to a more focused approach, students learn how to pronounce speech sounds and patterns correctly for an audience. They organize their thoughts, and develop fluency. Students learn to select appropriate words and sentences according to the proper social setting, audience, situation and subject matter. They learn how to organize their thoughts in a meaningful and logical sequence.

Debating

Students present evidence and improve argumentation skills. They develop confidence even when English is not the first language. Debate boosts aspirations, confidence and cultural awareness. As evidenced through students' perceptions and by an Ofsted review into creative approaches to learning, we find that classroom debates help students develop confidence and teach them to present their ideas more clearly

Use of English

End of week grammar lesson prepared to suit the student’s needs based on the teachers observations and discussion with the students in the previous days with the main focus on the use of English in practical situations.

Cultural immersion

Living in host families and going on excursions to iconic places complete the learning experience solidifying the further the language . The excursions are planned for during the lessons so students are prepared with the necessary vocabulary and expressions needed to understand and learn from the them.

*Program details and inclusions are subject to change.

FAQ

  1. How many weeks are your summer courses?
    You can study for a minimum of 2 weeks with us at Klace. Klace runs for 4 weeks in the Summer Holidays from July 23 - August 19. You can book for a maximum of 4 weeks.
  2. What dates are your courses ?
    • July 23 - August 5
    • July 30 - August 12
    • August 6 - August 19
  3. How many students are there per class?
    On average there are 12 students in a class, with a maximum of 15.
  4. How far in advance should I book my program?
    In order to have the widest choice accommodation, we recommend you book as far in advance as possible. If you book fewer than 30 days in advance you might incur a late booking fee.
  5. What kind of accommodation is offered
    • Accommodation will be with a host family that speaks only english
    • Two students per family, ideally students will have known each other
    • Host Families will have been CRB(Criminal Records Bureau) checked
  6. What do I need to know for my first day?
    When you arrive at your accommodation, you will receive a package with all the information you need for your English course. On your first day, you must bring in your Klace documents, your passport, proof of insurance and a pen. Your information package will tell you what time you need to be at school. On Day One, all students take an English placement test, meet the school staff and get an introduction to the school, facilities, courses and the local area. You will receive an individual timetable later in the day and your classes begin on the following day. If you have questions feel free to ask! The school staff is there to help.
  7. Do you offer courses for students of all English levels?
    Klace offers beginner to upper intermediate classes, and we also have basic elementary courses.
  8. Do I get a certificate or diploma after the course?
    You will receive a Certificate of Attendance at the end of your course.
  9. What does my English level need to be to study with Klace?
    For our courses at Klace, you can be any level of English.
  10. How old do I have to be to go to Klace London Summer School?
    For Klace summer school you need to be minimum 12 years old.
  11. How will Klace confirm my English language level?
    On your first day of class, you will take an English test on your grammar, writing, listening and speaking skills. Your class level will be based on your test results.
  12. When should I arrive at my destination?
    Classes start on Mondays, so we recommend you arrive on the Saturday or Sunday before your start date. Arrival of flights will between 11am and 5pm. The time span for the arrival of students is subject to changes.
  13. Should I be covered by insurance?
    You should have valid medical insurance for your entire stay.
  14. Does Klace accept Group Bookings?
    Yes, Klace does take full groups of students for courses. For more information, please contact us directly by email: info@klaceenglish.com
  15. Will there be someone to meet and greet me or us at the airport?
    Yes
  16. Is Airport transfer included in the price?
    No
  17. How can I pay for my course?
    You can online (website to be updated), pay over the phone or bank transfer.
  18. Can I pay in installments?
    You may pay in installments. The first payment is 50% and must be paid by April 30, 2017. The remainder of the fee must be paid May 30, 2017.
  19. The summer school and accommodation is located in Kingston Upon Thames. Is Kingston Upon Thames a safe area?
    Kingston Upon Thames is a very safe, upmarket and family oriented suburbs in Greater London.
